MUST TRY WHISKEYS FOR 2019

WhistlePig PiggyBack

With 2019 coming to a close what better way to close out the year than to try one of 3 must-try whiskeys of 2019. The first on our list is from Vermont craft whiskey producer Whistlepig and their late Master Distiller Dave Pickerell, formally of Marker's Mark.

This Estate Production distillery offers several award-winning products and although PiggyBack is a somewhat younger (6 years), weaker (96.56 proof) and less expensive whiskey (~ $50) than the brand’s high-end rye, it's an all-around better value.

Don't let the budding 6-year age fool you because, as Cindy Capparelli points out in the Whiskey Review , "[PiggyBack is] In fact, a combination of several batches from six to eight years old, with a weighted average of 7.25 years.“

This spiritus frumenti is a 100% red-hued barrel-aged rye version known as “Old Monongahela". This is, In fact, the only super-premium whiskey combining at least 6 years of age with high proof and a 100 percent rye grain bill.
